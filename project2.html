About Expresso

Expresso was my first college startup project. I have been a part of the team since it's beginning at Startup Weekend. Since Startup Weekend, I have acted as the platform’s user experience designer and have worked closely with a team of 10 Cal Poly Students. Over the past 3 years, we have developed a fully functioning preordering platform that includes an iOS application, an Android application, an iPad application, as well as an online web portal.

Startup Weekend 2013

Expresso was created at StartUp Weekend 2013 in San Luis Obispo, CA and has been growing ever since. At the competition, Expresso placed in 2nd Place and sparked the interest of not only the judges but of the team as well. Expresso is currently being worked on by a team of 10 Cal Poly students (including myself) and we hope to launch the product soon. Since the competition, Expresso has widened it’s target market from solely independent coffee shops to really any independant shop that wants to integrate a seamless preordering experience.

Expresso Mobile Application Design

Starting with the Expresso iOS application, my focus was to design an app that was both easy-to-use as well as visually pleasing. Through 3 years of user testing and development, we landed on our current application design. Soon after, we ported the design of the Expresso iOS application to Android and material design.

Expresso iPad Barista Application

When a customer orders from Expresso's mobile application, where do the orders go? The Expresso Barista application! While designing the Barista companion application, my focus was to find a way to convey to store employees how many customers had placed orders at the given time, how far those orders were from their desired pickup time, as well as give a way for employees to give customers an update on where their order was in the queue.
